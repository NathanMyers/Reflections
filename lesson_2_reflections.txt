What happens when you initialize a repository? Why do you need to do it?

Initializing a repository creates the initial stages of a repository so you can view what you are about to committ to version control and what you are not. You need to do it so you dont accidentally upload the wrong file(s). 

How is the staging area different from the working directory and the repository? What value do you think it offers?

Gives you a bit more leeway and an extra buffer against mistakes. 

How can you use the staging area to make sure you have one commit per logical change?

Because the staging area is structured in such a way to allow for one logical change.
